Anti-Money Laundering

Money laundering poses a serious threat to our economy and society. Criminals funnel billions of dollars through illegal channels, harming legitimate businesses and fueling further crimes. Together, we can identify and prevent suspicious activities before they wreak havoc.

Find Money Launderers

What is Money Laundering?

Money laundering is the process of disguising illegally obtained money so that it appears to come from legitimate sources. It often involves complex layers of transactions, sometimes across international borders, to obscure the origin of funds.

Why It Matters

Illicit financial flows undermine the integrity and stability of financial institutions, eroding trust and damaging global economies. By stopping money laundering, we reduce crime, protect legitimate businesses, and help ensure a fairer society for everyone.

Solution Overview

Over 1.5 million individual transactions have been meticulously analyzed using a combination of clustering, anomaly detection (autoencoders), and rule-based filtering. This layered approach flags potentially suspicious transactions with high accuracy, while still allowing a human agent to make the final determination on which transactions require further investigation.
